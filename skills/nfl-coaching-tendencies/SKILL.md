# NFL Head Coach Tendencies — 2026 Season

> **Usage note for BETTING agent:** Read the relevant team section before
> building any matchup analysis. New-hire coaches (⭐ NEW 2026) have limited
> HC ATS data — weight scheme/DC tendencies more heavily until a sample builds.

## AFC EAST

### Buffalo Bills — Joe Brady ⭐ NEW 2026
**Hired:** January 2026 | **Background:** Bills OC 2022–2025 → HC
**Record:** 0-0 (first HC season)
- **Offense:** Designed this Bills offense from the inside — Josh Allen usage, RPO mesh, designed QB runs baked into the system. Continuity is the Bills' biggest advantage in Year 1.
- **Defense:** Promoted from OC; DC TBD. Expect Bills to retain defensive identity from prior regime.

### New England Patriots — Mike Vrabel (2025)
**Hired:** January 2025 | **Background:** Titans HC 2018–2023 (54-45), Patriots LB great
**Record (NE):** 14-3, 1 AFC Championship (2025)
- **Offense:** Physical run game foundation, play-action off 12/21 personnel. Was adapting to Drake Maye's skill set in Year 1.
- **Defense:** Zone-coverage base, but disguises well. Forces opponents into check-downs and screens. Turnover culture.
- **4th down:** Conservative by analytics standards; situationally aggressive in late-game spots.
- **Clock management:** Elite. One of the best in-game managers in the league. Titans clock management was routinely praised.
- **ATS note:** 54-45 career. Titans covered 55% overall. NE 2025 covered 10 of 17 regular season games.
- **2026 context:** Year 2 of the Vrabel/Maye partnership. Expectations rise significantly — Patriots are a legitimate playoff threat.

### New York Jets — Aaron Glenn (2025)
**Hired:** January 2025 | **Background:** Lions DC 2021–2024
**Record (NYJ):** 3-14 (2025)
- **Offense:** Inherited a broken Jets infrastructure; QB situation was the root cause of 2025's record. Known as a defensive coach — offensive identity leans on OC.
- **Defense:** Press-man, Cover 1/Cover 3 blend, aggressive corner play. Dan Campbell/Lions defensive DNA.
- **4th down:** Defensive-minded HC; tends toward conventional on 4th.
- **Clock management:** Mixed in Year 1 — some poor late-game sequencing under pressure.
- **ATS note:** Jets went 5-12 ATS in 2025. Historically bad roster and QB situation inflated losses.
- **2026 context:** New QB situation is the defining variable. If the Jets address the position, Glenn's defensive competence becomes more visible.

### Cincinnati Bengals — Zac Taylor (2019)

### Pittsburgh Steelers — Mike McCarthy ⭐ NEW 2026
**Hired:** January 2026 | **Background:** Cowboys HC 2021–2025 (47-36), Packers HC 2006–2018 (125-77, Super Bowl XLV)
**Career record:** 172-113-2
- **Offense:** Pass-friendly system with physical run game complementing it. Aaron Rodgers/Dak Prescott type QB preferred. Will need to evaluate Pittsburgh's QB situation.

- **2026 context:** Inherits Pittsburgh post-Tomlin (retired after 18 seasons). Cultural reset for the franchise. McCarthy must re-establish toughness culture while modernizing the offense.

### Betting implications for new hires (2026)
- **Fade new HCs at home in Weeks 1-4** — installation lag is real. Market often overvalues "hot hire" new coaches.
- **Joe Brady (BUF) is the exception** — internal promotion, zero learning curve on offense, Josh Allen continuity.
- **John Harbaugh (NYG)** — elite HC pedigree but roster is a work in progress. Buy the culture; fade the record early.
- **Jesse Minter (BAL)** — Lamar carries this team. Minter's defensive pedigree is real. Don't underestimate Year 1.
- **Mike McCarthy (PIT)** — clock management concerns carry over. Pittsburgh identity requires toughness McCarthy hasn't always projected.
